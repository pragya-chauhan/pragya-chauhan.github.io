GPU Acceleration of Genome Read Mapping

This was my Master's Thesis for which I started with reading the bioinformatics and was drawn to the problem of mapping DNA sequences after they are read by the sequence generator. This introduced me to the world of Genome Analysis which incorporates software and hardware development beautifully. It required extreme dedication and hard work. But it was rewarding to research an application of computer engineering which impacts humans greatly.

Thesis Background

Mapping DNA reads against a reference genome remains to be a major bottleneck in the efficiency of DNA sequencers. Due to the massive length of genomic data, genome sequencing remains time-consuming. The existing algorithms have reached a bottleneck in performance and efforts are being made to optimize these using existing heterogeneous systems like multi-core processors, GPUs and FPGAs.

Approach for the Solution

Since I have a concentration in hardware engineering, I was interested in exploring the power of GPUs in one of my projects. GPUs with their parallel processing and high speeds appear as a promising solution to this problem. My research explores the acceleration potential of pattern matching algorithms used in genome sequencers on a GPU. Results I focused on implementing Knuth-Morris-Pratt (KMP) and Boyer-Moore algorithms sequentially on a CPU using C++ and parallelly on a GPU using CUDA C. From the results obtained I observed that parallel computing of algorithms results in better performance as the execution time is lesser compared to sequential computing. I obtained a speedup of 13 for KMP and 7 for the Boyer Moore algorithm. This serves as a prospect for future research in exploiting GPU computing to solve the read mapping problem.
